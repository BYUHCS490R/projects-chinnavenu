  • What are special entity characters?

    • special entity characters

    Special entity characters are characters that have a special meaning in HTML and cannot be used directly in the code. They are represented by a combination of characters that start with an ampersand (&) and end with a semicolon (;). For example, the less than symbol (<) is represented by < and the greater than symbol (>) is represented by >.

  • What are phrase tags?

    • Phrase tags are HTML tags that are used to style or emphasize text within a sentence (a phrase), without breaking the flow of the content. They are also called text-level semantic tags because they provide meaning and context to the enclosed text
