Structural Tags

Structural tags are HTML elements that define the structure and layout of a web page. They help organize content and provide semantic meaning to different sections of a webpage. Here are some common structural tags in HTML:

    What goes in the header?

  • <header>: Represents the header section of a webpage, typically containing the site title, logo, and navigation links.

    • What goes in the Nav? Can the Nav go in the header? Does it have to go in the header? Does the Nav contain links to another website?

  • <nav>: Defines a section of navigation links, usually used for menus or site navigation.

    • What goes in Main?

  • <main>: Represents the main content area of a webpage, typically containing the primary information or articles.

    • What goes in the Footer?

  • <footer>: Represents the footer section of a webpage, typically containing copyright information, contact details, and additional links.
